📐 O Padrão — W-Questions com Does
📊 Estrutura — W-word + does + he/she + verb?
W-worddoesSujeitoVerboExemplo
Whatdoeshe/shedo?What does Lucas do?
Wheredoeshe/shework?Where does she work?
What timedoeshe/shestart?What time does it start?
How longdoesit/he/shelast?How long does it last?
How oftendoeshe/shetravel?How often does she travel?
Howdoeshe/shetravel?How does Patricia travel?

💡 O verbo depois do sujeito é sempre a forma BASE — sem -s: travel, não travels.

⚠️ To Be vs Do/Does — não confunda
IS / ARE → identidade ou estado
  • Is he a coordinator? → cargo
  • Is she at the farm? → localização
  • Is the meeting at 9? → horário
DOES → ação habitual
  • Does he manage the team? → ação
  • Does she travel every week? → hábito
  • What does he do? → função
💬 Diálogo — James × Ana na AgraBrasil
🗣️ James chega à AgraBrasil · Segunda-feira, 8h45

🟡 amarelo = novo W7  ·  🔵 azul = revisão (W6, W3...)

James 🇦🇺
Good morning, Ana! What time does the tour of the farm start?
Ana 🇧🇷
Good morning! It starts at half past nine. Lucas arrives at quarter to nine — he coordinates the field team.
James 🇦🇺
What does Lucas do exactly? Does he manage crop quality too?
Ana 🇧🇷
Yes, he does! His main role is crop planning and quality. He always arrives early — never late.
James 🇦🇺
And Patricia — where does she work? Does she work here at headquarters?
Ana 🇧🇷
No, she doesn't. Her shift starts at half past five. She's based at the Santos port on Tuesdays.
James 🇦🇺
How long does her shift last? And how often does she travel to Santos?
Ana 🇧🇷
Her shift lasts about eight hours — she usually finishes at two o'clock. She travels every Tuesday, and sometimes on Thursdays too.

📊 How Questions — Perguntas e Respostas-Padrão
PerguntaResposta padrão
How long does it last?It lasts about thirty minutes.
How often does she travel?Every Tuesday.
How does he travel?He travels by car.
How long does it take?It takes about two hours.
Who does she manage?She manages the logistics team.
❌ Erros Clássicos — Short Answers
❌ Errado
  • Yes, he DO.
  • No, she DON'T.
  • Yes, I does.
  • Yes, we DOES.
  • No, they DOESN'T.
✅ Correto
  • Yes, he/she DOES.
  • No, he/she DOESN'T.
  • Yes, I/you/we/they DO.
  • No, we DON'T.
  • No, they DON'T.
🔑 Regra rápida:
PronomePositivoNegativo
he / she / itDOESDOESN'T
I / you / we / theyDODON'T
